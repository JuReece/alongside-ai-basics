# AI Basics Cheat Sheet
*Your Quick Reference Guide to Artificial Intelligence*

---

## What is AI?

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is technology that can:
- Understand and generate human language
- Recognize patterns in data
- Make predictions and suggestions
- Learn from examples

**Think of it as:** Very smart autocomplete that has read millions of books, articles, and websites.

---

## Common AI Terms

| Term | What It Means |
|------|---------------|
| **ChatGPT** | AI chatbot by OpenAI - like texting with a smart assistant |
| **Claude** | AI assistant by Anthropic - designed to be helpful, harmless, and honest |
| **Prompt** | The question or instruction you give to AI |
| **Large Language Model (LLM)** | The technology behind AI chatbots - trained on huge amounts of text |
| **Hallucination** | When AI makes up false information confidently |
| **Training Data** | The information AI learned from (text, images, etc.) |

## What AI CAN Do

✅ Write and edit text (emails, letters, essays)
✅ Explain complex topics simply
✅ Brainstorm ideas and solve problems
✅ Translate languages
✅ Summarize long documents
✅ Create study guides and learning materials
✅ Generate code and debug programs
✅ Answer general knowledge questions

---

## What AI CANNOT Do

❌ Access the internet in real-time (unless specifically enabled)
❌ Remember previous conversations (each chat is fresh)
❌ Know personal information about you
❌ Replace professional advice (medical, legal, financial)
❌ Understand emotions or context perfectly
❌ Always be 100% accurate

---

## Safety Rules

### 🔴 NEVER Share:
- Full names and addresses
- Phone numbers or email addresses
- Passwords or login credentials
- Bank account or credit card details
- Social Security or National Insurance numbers
- Medical record numbers
- School or workplace names with identifying details

### 🟢 ALWAYS:
- Fact-check important information
- Use AI as a first draft, not final answer
- Teach children to think critically
- Review before sharing AI-generated content
- Remember: AI can make mistakes

---

## The Golden Rule

> **AI is your first draft, not your final answer.**
>
> Always review, verify, and add your own thinking!

## Getting Started

1. **Choose a Platform**
   - Claude.ai (our recommendation)
   - ChatGPT
   - Other AI assistants

2. **Start Simple**
   - Try: "Explain [topic] to me like I'm 10 years old"
   - Or: "Help me write an email about [situation]"

3. **Be Specific**
   - Vague: "Help me plan dinner"
   - Better: "Suggest a healthy dinner for 4 people, vegetarian, budget £20"

4. **Iterate**
   - If the first response isn't perfect, ask follow-up questions
   - "Can you make it shorter/longer/simpler?"
   - "Can you focus more on [specific aspect]?"

---

## Example Prompts to Try

**For Writing:**
"Help me write a polite email to [person] about [topic]. Keep it professional but friendly."

**For Learning:**
"Explain how [concept] works in simple terms with a real-world example."

**For Planning:**
"Create a weekly meal plan for [number] people with [dietary requirements] and budget of [amount]."

**For Problem-Solving:**
"I need to [goal] but I'm struggling with [obstacle]. What are some strategies?"

---

## Red Flags - When AI Gets It Wrong

🚩 **Claims something is 100% certain** (nothing is!)
🚩 **Provides very specific numbers** without sources
🚩 **Gives medical/legal advice** (needs professional verification)
🚩 **Contradicts itself** in the same conversation
🚩 **Can't explain its reasoning** clearly
