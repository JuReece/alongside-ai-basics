# AI for Homework: Parent Guide
*Supporting Learning Without Doing the Work for Them*

---

## The Parent's Dilemma

You want to help your child with homework, but:
- You don't remember the maths you learned years ago
- You're unsure how they're being taught now
- You want them to learn, not just get answers
- You're worried about them using AI to cheat

**This guide will help you use AI as a learning tool, not a shortcut.**

---

## The Golden Rule

> **AI explains HOW, children do the WORK.**

AI should be like a tutor who guides understanding, not a service that does homework for them.

---

## What's Allowed vs. Cheating

### ✅ ALLOWED - Using AI to Learn:

**Understanding Concepts:**
- "Explain photosynthesis in simple terms"
- "What does 'metaphor' mean with examples?"
- "How do I solve fractions with different denominators?"

**Getting Unstuck:**
- "I don't understand this step in my maths problem. Can you explain the process?"
- "What's a good way to start an essay about Shakespeare?"

**Checking Work:**
- "Is my understanding of this concept correct?"
- "Does my essay structure make sense?"

---

### ❌ NOT ALLOWED - Cheating:

**Having AI Do the Work:**
- "Write my essay about World War 2"
- "Solve these 10 maths problems for me"
- "Give me the answers to these questions"

**Copying Without Understanding:**
- Copy-pasting AI responses
- Using AI words without comprehension
- Submitting AI work as their own

---

## How to Use AI for Different Subjects

### 📐 Maths

**Good Prompts:**
```
"Explain how to multiply fractions using an example"
"What's the process for solving 2x + 5 = 13?"
"Why do we need to find a common denominator?"
```

**Your Role:**
- Have them explain the method back to you
- Watch them solve similar problems
- Ensure they understand, don't just copy steps

**Red Flag:** They get the right answer but can't explain how

---

### 📚 English / Reading

**Good Prompts:**
```
"What are the main themes in [book title]?"
"Help me understand what this poem means"
"What's the difference between a simile and metaphor?"
```

**Your Role:**
- Discuss the concepts together
- Ask them what they think
- Have them write in their own words

**Red Flag:** Their writing style suddenly changes drastically

---

### 🔬 Science

**Good Prompts:**
```
"Explain the water cycle like I'm 10 years old"
"What's the difference between acids and bases?"
"Help me understand how plants make food"
```

**Your Role:**
- Ask them to explain it back
- Connect to real-world examples
- Do hands-on activities when possible

**Red Flag:** They can't answer basic questions about the topic

---

### 🌍 History / Geography

**Good Prompts:**
```
"Why did World War 1 start? Explain the main causes"
"What's the climate like in rainforests and why?"
"Who was [historical figure] and what did they do?"
```

**Your Role:**
- Verify facts with reputable sources
- Discuss different perspectives
- Encourage critical thinking

**Red Flag:** Information that sounds too detailed or perfect

## The Learning Conversation Method

Use this approach for any homework help:

### Step 1: Understand the Question
- "What is your homework asking you to do?"
- Make sure they understand what's being asked

### Step 2: What They Already Know
- "What do you already know about this topic?"
- Activate prior knowledge

### Step 3: Use AI as a Tutor
- Together, ask AI to explain the concept
- Read and discuss the response

### Step 4: Check Understanding
- "Can you explain that in your own words?"
- "Why does that work?"

### Step 5: Apply It Themselves
- They do the actual work
- You/AI provide hints if stuck, not answers

### Step 6: Review Together
- "Does your answer make sense?"
- "What did you learn?"

## Example: Homework Help Session

**Scenario:** Year 5 child struggling with fractions homework

### ❌ Wrong Way:
Parent: "Let me just show you..." (does the problem)
Or: "Ask ChatGPT to solve these"

### ✅ Right Way:

**Parent:** "What's the first problem?"

**Child:** "I need to add 1/4 + 1/3 but they have different denominators"

**Parent:** "Right. What do you know about adding fractions?"

**Child:** "They need the same bottom number?"

**Parent:** "Good! Let's ask AI to explain HOW to find a common denominator."

*Together, they ask AI:*
"Explain how to find a common denominator when adding fractions, using 1/4 + 1/3 as an example. Don't give me the answer, just explain the method."

*AI explains the process*

**Parent:** "OK, can you explain that back to me in your own words?"

**Child:** *Explains the process*

**Parent:** "Great! Now you try the problem using that method."

**Child:** *Works through it*

**Parent:** "Excellent! Now try the next one on your own."

## Red Flags Your Child is Cheating

Watch for these warning signs:

🚩 **Sudden Perfect Work**
- Previously struggled, now everything is perfect
- Vocabulary they wouldn't normally use
- Knowledge beyond what they've been taught

🚩 **Can't Explain Their Work**
- "I don't know, I just found it"
- Can't do similar problems
- Doesn't understand their own answers

🚩 **Suspicious Behaviour**
- Hiding screens when you approach
- Finishing homework unrealistically fast
- Resistant to showing their process

🚩 **AI-Style Writing**
- Overly formal or perfect grammar
- Advanced vocabulary out of nowhere
- Generic, essay-like responses

---

## What to Do If They Cheat

### Don't Panic
- It's a learning moment
- Most kids try it at some point
- Handle it calmly

### Have the Conversation

**Step 1: Private, Calm Discussion**
"I noticed your homework seems different from your usual work. Can we talk about it?"

**Step 2: Listen**
"Why did you feel you needed to do this?"
- Pressure to perform?
- Didn't understand?
- Saw friends doing it?

**Step 3: Explain Why It Matters**
- They're only cheating themselves
- Won't learn the material
- Tests will be harder
- Builds bad habits

**Step 4: Reset Expectations**
"Let's do this assignment together properly. I'll help you learn."

**Step 5: Create Better Habits**
- Set up proper AI use rules
- Regular check-ins
- Supervised homework time (temporary)

### Inform the School?

**Consider telling if:**
- Major assignment or exam
- Pattern of behaviour
- School's academic integrity policy

**You can also:**
- Help them redo the work properly
- Learn from the mistake
- Move forward with better habits

## Setting Up Good Habits

### Create a Homework Routine

**The Environment:**
- Common area, not bedroom
- AI device visible to you
- Regular check-in times

**The Process:**
1. Read assignment together
2. Discuss what they know
3. Identify what they need to learn
4. Use AI for understanding (together)
5. They do the actual work
6. Review together

**The Rules:**
- AI for learning, not answers
- Show your working
- Explain your thinking
- Ask for help when stuck

## Age-Appropriate Guidelines

### Primary School (Ages 5-11):
- **Always supervised** AI use
- Sit with them during homework
- Simple explanation prompts only
- Focus on understanding concepts
- Lots of hands-on practice

### Secondary School (Ages 11-16):
- **Monitored** AI use
- Check in regularly
- Teach proper research skills
- Random spot checks
- They explain their process to you

### Sixth Form (Ages 16-18):
- **Guided independence**
- Open conversations about integrity
- Teach citation and attribution
- Trust with verification
- Prepare for university expectations

## Prompts That Encourage Learning

### For Understanding:
- "Explain [concept] in simple terms with an example"
- "What's the difference between [A] and [B]?"
- "Why does [thing] work this way?"

### For Stuck Points:
- "I'm stuck on this step: [describe problem]. Can you explain the process without giving me the answer?"
- "What method should I use to solve [type of problem]?"

### For Checking:
- "Is my understanding of [concept] correct?"
- "Does this approach make sense for [problem]?"

### For Structure:
- "What should I include in an essay about [topic]?"
- "How do I structure a science report?"

## Working WITH Teachers

### Communication is Key:

**Tell Teachers:**
- Your family uses AI for homework help
- How you're using it (learning vs cheating)
- Ask about their AI policy

**Ask Teachers:**
- What's allowed and not allowed?
- How can AI support learning?
- What are the school's rules?

### If School Has Concerns:

**Be Honest:**
- "We use AI to explain concepts"
- "I supervise all AI use"
- "Happy to demonstrate our process"

**Work Together:**
- Follow school guidelines
- Adjust approach if needed
- Regular updates on progress

---

## Building Real Understanding

### The "Can They?" Test:

After using AI to help, check if they can:

□ Explain the concept in their own words
□ Solve similar problems independently
□ Teach it to someone else
□ Remember it the next day
□ Apply it in different contexts

**If NO to any:** More learning needed!
**If YES to all:** They genuinely understand!

---

## When Your Child Needs More Help

AI can't replace:

❌ Learning difficulties support
❌ Tutoring for ongoing struggles
❌ Teacher feedback
❌ Educational assessments

**If they're consistently struggling:**
- Talk to their teacher
- Consider professional tutoring
- Get learning needs assessed
- AI is a tool, not a solution

---

## Quick Reference: AI Homework Dos and Don'ts

### ✅ DO:
- Use AI to explain concepts
- Discuss AI responses together
- Have them do the actual work
- Check they understand
- Teach critical thinking
- Verify information
- Build on AI explanations

### ❌ DON'T:
- Let them copy AI answers
- Use AI unsupervised
- Skip understanding for speed
- Accept first response as truth
- Use AI for entire assignments
- Ignore school policies
- Assume AI is always right

## Final Thoughts

AI can be a powerful learning tool when used correctly. The key is:

1. **Supervision** - Especially for younger children
2. **Understanding** - Not just answers
3. **Integrity** - Doing their own work
4. **Communication** - Open dialogue about AI use

Your involvement makes all the difference. By teaching them to use AI responsibly now, you're preparing them for a future where AI will be everywhere.

**Remember:** The goal is learning, not perfect homework!
