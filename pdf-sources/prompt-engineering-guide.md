# Prompt Engineering for Beginners
*How to Write Better Prompts and Get Better AI Responses*

---

## What is a Prompt?

A **prompt** is the question or instruction you give to an AI system. Think of it as a conversation starter with a very knowledgeable assistant.

**Example:**
- ❌ Vague: "Help me"
- ✅ Good: "Help me write a friendly email to my child's teacher about extending a homework deadline"

---

## Why Good Prompts Matter

Better prompts = Better responses!

**Bad Prompt:** "Food ideas"
**AI Response:** *Generic list of recipes*

**Good Prompt:** "Suggest 3 healthy dinner ideas for a family of 4, including one vegetarian child, budget £20, taking 30 minutes or less"
**AI Response:** *Specific, useful suggestions matching your exact needs*

---

## The 5 Elements of a Great Prompt

### 1. **Context** - Give Background
Who you are, what situation you're in

**Example:**
"I'm a parent of a 10-year-old who struggles with focus..."

---

### 2. **Task** - Be Specific
What exactly do you want

**Example:**
"...help me create a homework routine that works for children with ADHD..."

---

### 3. **Format** - How You Want It
List, paragraph, step-by-step, table, etc.

**Example:**
"...as a simple visual checklist..."

---

### 4. **Constraints** - Set Boundaries
Length, tone, level, time, budget

**Example:**
"...that takes no more than 45 minutes total..."

---

### 5. **Examples** - Show What You Mean
Provide examples of what you want

**Example:**
"...similar to the one attached, but with more specific timings"

## The Before & After: Real Examples

### Example 1: Meal Planning

❌ **Weak Prompt:**
"Meal ideas"

✅ **Strong Prompt:**
"Create a weekly dinner menu for a family of 4 (2 adults, 2 children ages 6 and 9). One child is vegetarian. Budget: £75. Must include nutritional balance and use common ingredients from UK supermarkets. Provide a shopping list organized by supermarket section."

**Why it's better:**
- Context: family size and ages
- Specific requirements: vegetarian, budget
- Constraints: UK ingredients
- Format requested: organized shopping list

---

### Example 2: Email Writing

❌ **Weak Prompt:**
"Write an email"

✅ **Strong Prompt:**
"Help me write a polite but firm email to my internet provider. Context: We've had 3 outages this month, I've reported each one. I want a partial refund and assurance this will be fixed. Tone: professional but assertive. Length: 3 short paragraphs maximum."

**Why it's better:**
- Context: specific problem
- Task: clear outcome wanted
- Tone specified
- Length constraint

---

### Example 3: Homework Help

❌ **Weak Prompt:**
"Explain photosynthesis"

✅ **Strong Prompt:**
"Explain photosynthesis to a 9-year-old in simple terms. Use a real-world analogy they'd understand. Include the main steps but avoid complex scientific terms. Follow with 3 questions I can ask my child to check they understood."

**Why it's better:**
- Audience specified: 9-year-old
- Method: analogy
- Constraints: simple language
- Extra request: check questions

## Prompt Formulas That Work

### Formula 1: The Clear Request
```
"I need to [TASK] for [AUDIENCE].
Please [SPECIFIC ACTION]
in a [TONE/STYLE] way.
Keep it [CONSTRAINT]."
```

**Example:**
"I need to explain budgeting to my teenager. Please create a simple guide in a casual, non-preachy way. Keep it under 500 words."

---

### Formula 2: The Problem-Solver
```
"I'm facing [PROBLEM] in [SITUATION].
My goal is [DESIRED OUTCOME].
Suggest [NUMBER] practical solutions
considering [CONSTRAINTS]."
```

**Example:**
"I'm facing difficulty getting my 7-year-old to bed on time during school nights. My goal is a consistent 8pm bedtime. Suggest 5 practical solutions considering we have limited evening time and he has lots of energy."

---

### Formula 3: The Learning Helper
```
"Explain [CONCEPT] to someone who [KNOWLEDGE LEVEL].
Use [TEACHING METHOD].
Include [ADDITIONAL REQUESTS]."
```

**Example:**
"Explain fractions to someone who understands basic division. Use visual examples with pizza or pie. Include 3 practice problems that gradually increase in difficulty."

---

### Formula 4: The Creative Brief
```
"Create [DELIVERABLE] for [PURPOSE].
Style: [TONE/STYLE]
Include: [SPECIFIC ELEMENTS]
Avoid: [THINGS TO EXCLUDE]"
```

**Example:**
"Create a birthday party plan for my 8-year-old's Harry Potter themed party. Style: magical and fun but achievable. Include: games, decorations we can make at home, menu, timeline. Avoid: expensive store-bought items or things requiring special skills."

## Power Words That Improve Prompts

### For Better Explanations:
- "Explain like I'm 5/10/a beginner..."
- "Break this down into simple steps..."
- "Use an everyday analogy..."
- "What's a real-world example of..."

### For Better Structure:
- "Organize this as..."
- "Create a step-by-step guide..."
- "Format this as a table/list/outline..."
- "Structure this from most to least important..."

### For Better Tone:
- "Write in a friendly/professional/casual tone..."
- "Make this more encouraging/direct/gentle..."
- "Keep the language simple/formal/conversational..."

### For Better Depth:
- "Give me 3 specific examples..."
- "Provide detailed reasoning..."
- "Include pros and cons..."
- "What are the main points in 3 bullet points..."

## The "Ask Me Questions" Trick

If you're not sure how to write a good prompt, try this:

**Your Prompt:**
"I need help planning a family holiday but I'm not sure what details you need. Please ask me questions to help create the perfect itinerary."

**AI will respond:**
"I'd be happy to help! Let me ask you some questions:
1. How many people? Ages?
2. Preferred destination type?
3. Budget range?
4. Duration?
5. Interests and activities?
..."

Then you answer, and AI creates exactly what you need!

## Common Prompt Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Too Vague
"Help me with dinner"

**Fix:** Be specific!
"Suggest a quick vegetarian dinner recipe for 2 that uses ingredients I likely have: pasta, tomatoes, cheese, onions, garlic"

---

### ❌ Mistake 2: Too Many Tasks at Once
"Help me plan my week, organize my finances, create a meal plan, and write emails"

**Fix:** One task at a time!
Start with: "Help me create a weekly meal plan. Once we finish that, I'll ask about the other tasks."

---

### ❌ Mistake 3: Assuming AI Knows Context
"How do I fix it?"

**Fix:** Provide context!
"My dishwasher isn't draining properly. Water sits at the bottom after a cycle. What are common causes and how can I fix each one?"

---

### ❌ Mistake 4: Not Specifying Tone/Style
"Write a complaint letter"

**Fix:** Specify tone!
"Write a firm but polite complaint letter about faulty goods, requesting a full refund. Professional tone, 3 paragraphs maximum."

---

### ❌ Mistake 5: Forgetting Your Audience
"Explain quantum physics"

**Fix:** Specify level!
"Explain quantum physics to a curious 12-year-old who loves science. Use simple analogies and avoid complex maths."

## Iterating: Making Responses Better

First response not quite right? **Refine it!**

### Round 1:
**You:** "Give me birthday party ideas for a 6-year-old"

**AI:** *Gives generic list*

### Round 2:
**You:** "Make these more specific for a dinosaur-loving child. Include activities that work indoors since it's a December party. Budget: £100."

**AI:** *Gives better, tailored ideas*

### Round 3:
**You:** "Perfect! Can you now create a timeline for the 2-hour party, including setup time?"

**AI:** *Provides detailed schedule*

**Key Point:** It's a conversation! Ask follow-ups to refine.

## Prompt Templates for Families

### Template 1: Homework Help
```
"My [AGE]-year-old is learning about [TOPIC] in school.
Explain [SPECIFIC CONCEPT] in simple terms
using [TYPE OF EXAMPLE].
Then give me 3 questions to check understanding."
```

---

### Template 2: Meal Planning
```
"Create a [NUMBER] day meal plan for [FAMILY SIZE].
Dietary needs: [REQUIREMENTS].
Budget: [AMOUNT].
Include [SPECIFIC REQUESTS: easy recipes/shopping list/prep time].
Avoid [DISLIKES/ALLERGIES]."
```

---

### Template 3: Activity Planning

```

---

### Template 4: Email Writing
```

## The Follow-Up Questions to Always Ask

After getting an AI response, improve it with:

1. **"Can you make this simpler?"**
   - Reduces complexity

2. **"Can you give me a specific example?"**
   - Makes it more concrete

3. **"What are the pros and cons of this approach?"**
   - Adds critical thinking

4. **"Are there any alternatives?"**
   - Gives you options

5. **"Can you format this as [LIST/TABLE/STEPS]?"**
   - Makes it easier to use

## Privacy-Safe Prompt Rewrites

Never share personal details! Rewrite prompts to be safe:

### ❌ Unsafe:
"My daughter Emma, age 8, at Willow Primary School in Leeds is being bullied by a classmate named Sophie"

### ✅ Safe:
"My 8-year-old is experiencing bullying from a classmate. How should I approach this with the school?"

---

### ❌ Unsafe:
"I live at 24 Oak Road, Manchester M13 4PQ. What's the best internet provider?"

### ✅ Safe:
"I'm in the Greater Manchester area. What internet providers have good service here?"

---

## Testing Your Prompts: The Checklist

Before sending your prompt, check:

□ **Is it specific enough?** (Who, what, when, where, why, how)
□ **Did I provide context?** (Background information)
□ **Did I specify format?** (How I want the response)
□ **Did I set constraints?** (Length, tone, level)
□ **Did I remove personal information?** (Privacy check)
□ **Is it one clear task?** (Not multiple requests)

## Advanced Tips

### Tip 1: Role Prompting
Ask AI to take on a specific role:

"Act as a primary school teacher. Explain [concept] to a 7-year-old..."

"Act as a financial advisor. Review this family budget and suggest improvements..."

---

### Tip 2: Chain of Thought
Ask AI to show its reasoning:

"Explain your thinking step-by-step as you solve this problem..."

---

### Tip 3: Constraint Creativity
Constraints often make responses better:

"In exactly 100 words..."
"Using only common household items..."
"Without spending any money..."

**For Learning:**
- "Explain [topic] to a [age]-year-old..."
- "What's a simple way to understand..."
- "Break down [concept] into steps..."

- "Create a [timeframe] plan for [goal]..."

**For Writing:**
- "Help me write a [type] to [recipient] about [topic]..."

- "Draft a [document] that includes [elements]..."

**For Problems:**
- "I'm struggling with [issue]. Suggest solutions that..."
- "What are different approaches to [problem]..."
- "How can I [goal] while avoiding [obstacle]..."
