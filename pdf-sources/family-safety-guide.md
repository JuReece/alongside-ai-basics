# Family AI Safety Guide
*Using AI Safely and Responsibly with Your Family*

---

## Introduction

Welcome to the Family AI Safety Guide! This guide will help you and your family use AI tools safely, responsibly, and effectively. Whether you're new to AI or already using it, these guidelines will ensure everyone stays protected.

---

## The 3 Golden Rules

### 1. 🛡️ Protect Privacy
Never share personal information with AI systems.

### 2. ✅ Verify Everything
AI can make mistakes - always fact-check important information.

### 3. 🧠 Think Critically
Use AI to enhance your thinking, not replace it.

---

## What NOT to Share with AI

### 🔴 NEVER Share These:

**Personal Identifiers:**
- Full names combined with location
- Complete addresses (street, city, postcode)
- Phone numbers
- Email addresses (when paired with other info)
- Dates of birth
- Photos of faces

**Sensitive Information:**
- Passwords or PINs
- Bank account details
- Credit card numbers
- National Insurance / Social Security numbers
- Medical record numbers
- School or workplace IDs

**Private Details:**
- Medical conditions or diagnoses
- Mental health information
- Legal issues or court cases
- Financial problems or debt details
- Relationship conflicts with identifying details
- Children's full names, ages, and locations together

## Safe Ways to Rewrite Prompts

### ❌ UNSAFE:
"My son Jake, 7, at Riverside Primary School in Manchester is struggling with reading comprehension."

### ✅ SAFE:
"My 7-year-old is struggling with reading comprehension. What strategies can help?"

---

### ❌ UNSAFE:
"I live at 42 Oak Street, Birmingham B12 3XY. What's the best internet provider?"

### ✅ SAFE:
"I'm in the West Midlands area. What internet providers have good service here?"

---

### ❌ UNSAFE:
"My email is sarah.jones@email.com and I need help writing a complaint about my phone contract."

### ✅ SAFE:
"Help me write a complaint letter about a phone contract. I've had poor service and want a refund."

## Age-Appropriate AI Use

### Ages 5-8: Supervised Only
- **Always** sit with them
- Use child-friendly AI tools
- Explain: "The computer tries to help but can make mistakes"
- Focus on: creative stories, simple questions, fun learning

**Example Activities:**
- "Tell me a story about a friendly dragon"
- "What do elephants eat?"
- "Help me think of rhymes for 'cat'"

---

### Ages 9-12: Supervised with Guidance
- Sit nearby and check frequently
- Review all AI responses together
- Teach them to spot incorrect information
- Focus on: homework help (not doing it for them), creativity, research

**Teaching Moment:**
"Let's ask AI this question. Then let's look it up in a book or trusted website to check if it's right!"

**Approved Uses:**
- Explaining concepts they're learning
- Brainstorming creative writing ideas
- Practice spelling or math (but they do the work!)
- Learning about topics they're curious about

---

### Ages 13-16: Monitored Independence
- Set clear rules and expectations
- Regular check-ins about AI use
- Teach critical thinking and fact-checking
- Focus on: research, studying, appropriate social interaction

**Family Agreement Includes:**
- What AI can and can't be used for
- Privacy rules (their own and others')
- No cheating on schoolwork
- Come to parents with concerns

---

### Ages 16+: Responsible Use with Oversight
- Treat them as young adults
- Open conversations about AI ethics
- Guide on professional use (CVs, job applications)
- Discuss misinformation and AI-generated content

## Homework and AI: The Right Way

### ❌ WRONG WAY (Cheating):
"Write my essay about Shakespeare's Macbeth"

### ✅ RIGHT WAY (Learning):
"Explain the main themes in Macbeth so I can understand them better for my essay"

---

### The Learning Approach:

1. **Understand First**
   - Use AI to explain concepts
   - Ask "why" and "how" questions

2. **Get Ideas**
   - Brainstorm topics or approaches
   - Discuss different perspectives

3. **Do Your Own Work**
   - Write your own essays/answers
   - Show your own thinking

4. **Check and Improve**
   - Ask AI to review for clarity (not do it for you)
   - Get suggestions for improvement

## Recognizing AI Hallucinations

AI "hallucinations" are when AI confidently states false information. Here's how to spot them:

### 🚩 Red Flags:

1. **Overly Specific Claims**
   - "This happened on January 15, 2022 at 3:47 PM" (too precise without source)

2. **Made-up Sources**
   - "According to Dr. Smith's 2023 study..." (source doesn't exist)

3. **Impossible Events**
   - Dates that don't match (Shakespeare using computers)
   - Events that couldn't have happened

4. **Contradictions**
   - Says one thing, then contradicts itself
   - Different answer to same question

### ✅ How to Verify:

- Search reputable sources (BBC, NHS, .gov sites)
- Check multiple sources
- Ask: "Does this sound right?"
- When in doubt, ask a human expert

## Scams and Deepfakes

### AI-Generated Scams to Watch For:

**Fake Emails/Messages:**
- AI can write convincing phishing emails
- Check sender address carefully
- Look for requests for money or personal info

**Voice Cloning:**
- Scammers can clone voices from social media
- If "family member" calls asking for money, verify!
- Call them back on a known number
- Use a secret family code word

**Deepfake Videos:**
- AI can create fake videos of real people
- Check: lighting, lip sync, edges of face
- Verify through official channels
- Teach kids: "Seeing isn't always believing"

### Family Safety Protocol:

1. **Create a Code Word**
   - Only family knows it
   - Use if calling about emergencies/money

2. **Verify Emergency Calls**
   - Hang up
   - Call back on known number
   - Contact other family members

3. **Never Rush**
   - Scammers create urgency
   - Take time to verify

## When NOT to Use AI

AI should NEVER replace professional advice for:

### 🏥 Medical Issues
- Symptoms or diagnosis
- Medication questions
- Mental health crises
- Emergency situations

**Instead:** See your GP, call 111, or 999 for emergencies

---

### ⚖️ Legal Matters
- Legal advice or contracts
- Court issues
- Rights and responsibilities
- Official documents

**Instead:** Consult a solicitor or Citizens Advice

---

### 💰 Financial Decisions
- Investment advice
- Debt management
- Benefits questions
- Tax issues

**Instead:** See a financial advisor or accountant

---

### 🚨 Emergencies
- Medical emergencies
- Safety threats
- Crime reporting
- Urgent help needed

**Instead:** Call 999 or appropriate emergency service

## Red Flags for Parents

Watch for these warning signs:

🚩 Child is secretive about AI use
🚩 Sudden improvement in homework (possible cheating)
🚩 Spending excessive time with AI chatbots
🚩 Sharing personal information
🚩 Using AI for inappropriate content
🚩 Emotional attachment to AI assistant

**What to do:**
- Have a calm, non-judgmental conversation
- Explain why you're concerned
- Review and reinforce safety rules
- Consider supervised use for a while

---

## Privacy Settings and Controls

### On Devices:
- Use parental control apps
- Monitor browser history
- Set up screen time limits
- Create separate child accounts

### With AI Tools:
- Review each platform's privacy policy
- Opt out of data collection when possible
- Use temporary/incognito browsing
- Delete conversation history regularly

### At Home:
- AI devices in common areas (not bedrooms)
- Regular "tech audits" together
- Open door policy for questions

---

## Teaching Critical Thinking

Help your children question and think:

**Ask These Questions:**
- "How do you know that's true?"
- "What sources can we check?"
- "Does that make sense?"
- "What might AI have gotten wrong?"
- "Would a human expert agree?"

**Teach Them to:**
- Question everything
- Seek multiple sources
- Think for themselves
- Ask "Why?" and "How?"
- Trust their gut feelings
